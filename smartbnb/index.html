← smartbnb

Democratizing technology for short-term rental hosts.

I joined smartbnb - a fully distributed team, building the future of short-term rental industry - in late 2018 as a product designer to lead the visual direction and improve the user experience of the web app that's heavily used by short-term rental hosts worldwide.

After spending my first 6 months improving the current version and learning more about the userbase, I dived deep into building a new, more consistent and sustainable interface which allowed us not only fix the known usability problems but also leaving enough wiggle room for new features to come.

I took on the new branding and marketing website in addition to web and mobile app.

A first look at the dashboard, a welcoming screen that presents you a daily overview of your business at a glance.

Playful and bold branding, just like we wanted it to be.

A matching marketing website giving users obvious hints about the experience smartbnb offers.

Sustainable building blocks and solid visual hierarchy to keep the experience consistent across the app.

Simplified mobile experience without losing the functionality.

A detailed filtering system that allows the users to segment their data easily and create shortcuts for later use.

A sleek calendar that allows hosts to manage the availability and pricing of their properties visually.

Proactive interface that aims to inform the user where they need extra guidance.

Lots and lots of small details to make the user's life easier.

A complete data visualization tool to help users make data-driven decisions.

Meaningful breakdown of data without any extra effort.

Interactable components to learn more about certain things whenever needed.

The cool kid of the industry.

After nine months of long meetings, wonderful discussions and huge amounts of coffee, we launched smartbnb v.2 in autumn 2019.

The hard work we've put in the complete overhaul of a platform that is used daily by thousands of hosts has paid off not only in appraisal but also a significant jump in the MRR and NPS scores.

Working as a one-man-design-team in this project was both satisfying in terms of outcome and also one of the highlights of my life. 10/10 would do it again!
